Abstract

Social navigation in robotics primarily involves guiding mobile robots through human-populated areas, balancing pedestrian comfort with efficient path-finding. While this field has seen diverse approaches, a solution for seamless integration of robots into pedestrian settings remains elusive. In this paper, we develop a social force model to a quadruped legged robot, utilising visual perception for human localisation. In particular, we introduce an augmented social force model that incorporates refined interpretations of repulsive forces and avoidance behaviours based on pedestrian actions, alongside a target following mechanism. Extensive evaluations on a Boston Dynamics Spot quadruped legged robot, through various scenarios, including interactions with oncoming pedestrians, crowds, and obstructed paths, demonstrate that the proposed augmented model significantly improves upon the previous state-of-the-art methods in terms of chosen path length, average velocity, and time-to-goal for effective and efficient social navigation.
